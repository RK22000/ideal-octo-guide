**setting_fingerprint_driver_for_linux** (Subheading), authored by raka I dual booted my windows machine with Pop!OS today to try out [ZED](https://zed.dev/). While I had this I also fell in the rabbit hole of trying to enable the finger print reader for authentication. As I understood it from a little quacking around online, all I needed was `fprintd` and `libpam-fprintd`. Unfortunately my fingerprint reader is not included in the [list of suported devices](https://fprint.freedesktop.org/supported-devices.html). However thanks to the [Synaptics Tudor Driver Relinking Project](https://github.com/Popax21/synaTudor) my fingerprint scanner could be made to work with libfprint. Thanks Popax21 and everyone wo contributed to the project. Also thanks _u/16mhz_ for makeing the [post](https://www.reddit.com/r/openSUSE/comments/10xzlxo/using_synaptics_06cb00be_fingerprint_with/?rdt=40038) highlighting the project Thing to note.
